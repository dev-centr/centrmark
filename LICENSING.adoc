= Licensing and contribution intent

This repository is released under the link:https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.html[GNU Affero General Public License v3.0] (`AGPL-3.0`). The full legal terms are in the `LICENSE` file.

== Why AGPL

* **Forkable implementations.** Anyone may fork, study, and modify this work. That includes parsers, tooling, and documentation derived from this tree.
* **Improvements flow back.** If someone distributes a modified version - or, for Affero-covered programs, runs modified versions as a network service - they generally must offer corresponding source under the same license. That keeps enhancements visible to the community instead of disappearing into proprietary-only stacks.
* **Attention stays with upstream.** Commercial exploitation of *this codebase* still requires compliance with AGPL; it does not create a parallel proprietarized branch without source obligations.

== What copyright cannot do

Copyright and AGPL bind people who copy or derive from *this repository*. They do **not** stop independent teams from writing a clean-room parser or alternate specification from scratch without using our expressive fixed form—that is a limitation of any license.

To reduce confusion and "embrace, extend, extinguish" dynamics around naming and compatibility claims, see link:TRADEMARK.adoc[Trademark guidelines].

== Trademark

The names _CentrMark_, _CMK_, and the `.cmk` file extension are used here to identify this format and project. link:TRADEMARK.adoc[Trademark guidelines] describe permitted descriptive use and how forks should brand materially different variants.

If the relevant copyright holders have the necessary rights, they may offer an alternative licensing agreement for specific proprietary deployments that want to avoid AGPL corresponding source obligations.
